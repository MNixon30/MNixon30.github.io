Circular Motion of Water in a Barrel

1. Problem Definition
1.1 Problem

This study will look at the effect of rotation on a 1m high 1m diameter open top container which is half filled with water.

1.2 Hand Calculations of Expected Results

Calculation of maximum angular velocity before water begins to spill out of the container.

2. Setup
2.1 Geometry

Geometry modeled in ANSYS Spaceclaim by pulling a 1 meter diameter circle sketch to 1m in height. The 2 fluid zones were split using a plane passing at z = 0.5m so that fluent can fill half the container with water later in the simulation.

2.2 Meshing

Describe mesh details: size, refinement, and boundary layer elements if any.

2.3 Fluent Solver

Summarize Fluent settings: model selection (VOF, gravity, rotational reference frame), time step, and boundary conditions.

3. Results
3.1 40 RPM

Water begins to circulate with low angular velocity leading to a slight parabolic shape.

3.2 65 RPM

As the speed approaches the calculated value of 85rpm from section 1.2, peak water height increases alongside the trough depth.

3.3 80 RPM

The water should begin to reach the edges of the container around 85rpm (see section 1.2). However due to various errors in the model (see section 4) and various assumptions in the hand calculations (section 1.2), the actual rpm value which would result in overflow should be higher than the hand calculated value.

3.4 100 RPM

Running the calculation at 100rpm resulted in water becoming even closer to overflow. It is noted that the water does not reach the top of the container at this rpm. This indicates that the assumptions in the CFD model do not hold near the boundary between the container and the outlet(top), resulting in skewed results as the rpm is increased. More details on errors are discussed in section 4.

4. Errors

Discuss any simulation convergence issues, mesh problems, or mismatches between CFD and theoretical predictions.
