3. Results
3.1 40 RPM

Water begins to circulate with low angular velocity leading to a slight parabolic shape.

3.2 65 RPM

As the speed approches the calculated value of 85rpm from section 1.2, peak water height increases alongside the trough depth.

3.3 80 RPM

The water should begin to reach the edges of the container around 85rpm (see section 1.2). However due to various errors in the model (see section 4) and various assuimptions in the hand calculations (section 1.2), the actual rpm value which would result in overflow should be higher than the hand caluclasted value.

3.4 100 RPM

Running the calculation at 100rpm rersulted in water becomming even closer to overflow. It is noted that the water does not reach the top of the container at this rpm. This indicated that the assumptions in the CFD model do not hold near the boundry between the container and the outlet(top), resulting in skewed results as the rpm is increased. More details on errors are discussed in section 4.
