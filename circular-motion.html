Circular Motion of Water in a Barrel

1. Problem Definition
1.1 Problem

This study looks at the effect of rotation on a 1m high, 1m diameter open-top container half-filled with water.

1.2 Hand Calculations of Expected Results

Calculation of maximum angular velocity before water begins to spill out of the container.

2. Setup
2.1 Geometry

Geometry modeled in ANSYS Spaceclaim by pulling a 1-meter diameter circle sketch to 1m in height. The two fluid zones were split using a plane at z = 0.5m.

2.2 Meshing

Describe mesh details: size, refinement, and boundary layer elements if any.

2.3 Fluent Solver

Summarize Fluent settings: model selection (VOF, gravity, rotational reference frame), time step, and boundary conditions.

3. Results
3.1 40 RPM

Water begins to circulate with low angular velocity leading to a slight parabolic shape.

3.2 65 RPM

As the speed approaches 85 RPM, peak water height increases alongside the trough depth.

3.3 80 RPM

At 80 RPM, water approaches the top edge. Due to model assumptions and simplifications, real overflow occurs above calculated limits.

3.4 100 RPM

At 100 RPM, overflow nears. Results may diverge due to limitations in boundary modeling.

4. Errors

Discuss simulation convergence issues, mesh problems, and mismatches between CFD and theory.
